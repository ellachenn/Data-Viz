World Quality of Life

Background: The map and bar chart below visualize data on world quality of life. While the map shows the quality of life score by country, the bar chart shows the average quality of life in the world by continent.The data used is from two Kaggle datasets linked below. As shown through the sliders, the factors considered in the calculation of quality of life scores according to the Kaggle datasets are stability, rights, health, safety, climate, costs, and popularity.

Analysis: When all of the factors contributing to the quality of life score are weighed the same, Australia has the highest quality of life, and Canada, the US, Scandinavian countries, and some other European countries follow closely with high quality of life scores, as shown by the map. In the same scenario, the bar chart shows that Oceania has the highest quality of life score, followed by Europe. As the sliders change, the numbers and visuals change accordingly as well.

Instructions: The map and bar chart default to having all of the factors weighed equally, and therefore produce a visual accordingly. As the legend suggests, low to high quality of life scores are expressed in color from pink to turquoise to dark blue, respectively. Of course, everyone has different values and priorities, so change the sliders to calculate the quality of life of countries and continents according to what's most important to you!

Note: there is no data for countries in white.
